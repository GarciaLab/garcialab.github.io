The ability to measure the full complement of RNA molecules in space and time 
within individual cells—and to interpret the flood of data from these 
technologies—is revolutionising how we dissect biological functions. 
In this series of lectures, we will examine how physical modelling can interact 
with these new data types to reveal the molecular mechanisms behind transcription 
and its regulation. Participants will be introduced to the 
fundamentals of dynamical systems theory and chemical master equations 
to predict both the mean and distribution of mRNA over time. We will also 
develop a “theory of the experiment” for single-cell sequencing, 
exploring how to extract meaningful mechanistic insights from complex datasets. 
The sessions will combine pen-and-paper derivations with uncomplicated coding 
exercises in Google Colab, accessible from any laptop or tablet. 
No prior background in mathematics, physics, or programming is necessary.
